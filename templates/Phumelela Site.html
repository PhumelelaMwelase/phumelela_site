Early Life

“What do you call a man who was born in Jo’burg, grew up in Durban, and dies in Cape Town?”
"Dead."
That was an unfunny joke I heard on TV when I was 8-years-old. One which has to-date left me terrified of ever visiting the Mother City.
Ok maybe not terrified, but at least hesitent because so far, two thirds of that is my story. I was born in Jo’burg to two other cool people. Then, when I was 5-years-old, my mother decided to move the family to the coolest place in SA.

Adoloscence

I’ve lived most of my life in the small town of Amanzimtoti. I attended primary school and then high school a few kilometers from my house.
My first big disappointment was in 2014 when I wasn’t accepted into UKZN for a BCom in entrepreneurship because unsatisfactory Maths marks. No matter, I was able to participate in a short course hosted by the school that gave us the opportunity to prove we weren’t irredeemably hopeless.

Having completed the programme, I was accepted into the University where I studied for a year, learning a little about IT, Accounting, Management, and Commercial Law, all of which I enjoyed. I also studied Maths ... which I didn’t enjoy. Towards the end of that year my father, who wasn’t happy with the course of my career (he would’ve preferred I pursue something more traditional like teaching,) decided he would no longer pay for my education and I had to drop out.

2015 - 2016

Roots Design

In 2014 I had read Robert Kiyosaki’s Rich Dad Poor Dad, where he had encouraged taking on jobs for the educational value rather than the income. Having enjoyed my short stint in IT, and with the desire to help a start up, I decided to accept the opportunity to work for a Web Design company called Roots Design. While my responsibilties were more in the vein of data capturing and copy editing, this was where I was first introduced to Web and Graphic Design on platforms such as Wordpress and Photoshop. It was ultimately an enriching experience overall.

"The lesson is – don’t take a job for the money, take a job to learn a skill."
- Robert Kiyosaki, Rich Dad, Poor Dad

2017 - 2018

Internship

In 2017 I accepted an unpaid internship for a local church hoping to pilot the program to fine tune for post-matriculant youth. These was meant to foster skills development, and and prepare etc. I was able to give feedback on what was missing and what could useful. The pilot led to the church developing a much more comprehensive programme, and led to me being hired to manage the coffee shop in 2019.

2019 - 2020

Life Cafe

Towards the end of 2018 I was approached by the church I was attending who knew about my passion for business to manage the socially successful, but fiscally failing coffee shop.

Within 4 months, with the help of some amazing people, we were able to break even and justify it’s continued existence.

I learned how to learn from people who know better than me, and also how to win people over into a new way of doing things when “This is how we’ve always done things,” was no longer working.

2020 - 2022

KZN Print Media

Having learned what I could about turning a business around from Life Cafe, in 2020 I accepted the opportunity to be the first hire for a new printing company.
This was going to give me a hands on experience in learning what goes into starting a new venture.
I especially learned how to manage resources, handle customer relations, and unlike Life Cafe where I had little to no oversight, I learned how to break down management goals into smaller executable tasks.
