Adoloscence

I was born in Jo'burg, but have lived most of my life in the small town of 'Toti, where I attended primary school and then high school, all just a few kilometers from my house.
My first brush with failure was after high school when I wasn't invited to study a BCom at UKZN due to less-than-satisfactory Maths marks. It was completely fair, but it stung nonetheless.
I managed to turn it around after completing a short maths course in time to resubmit my application, where I was finally accepted.
For a year, I was happy to study IT, Accounting, Management, and Commercial Law, all of which I thoroughly enjoyed. I also had to study Maths, which I didn't enjoy, but c'est la vie. Towards the end of that year, my father, who would have preferred I pursue teaching, decided he would no longer pay for my education, and I was forced to drop out. But onward and upward.

2015 - 2016

Roots Design

In 2014, I read Robert Kiyosaki's "Rich Dad Poor Dad," in which he encouraged taking on jobs for educational value rather than income. Having enjoyed my short stint in IT, and with the desire to help a start-up, I decided to accept the opportunity to work for a web design company called Roots Design. While my responsibilities were more in the vein of data capturing and copy editing, this was where I was first introduced to web and graphic design on platforms such as WordPress and Photoshop. It was ultimately an enriching experience overall.
Not to mention, the owner was really cool.

"The lesson is – don’t take a job for the money, take a job to learn a skill."
- Robert Kiyosaki, Rich Dad, Poor Dad

2017 - 2018

Internship

Continuing in my pursuit of workplace-based education, in 2017 I accepted an unpaid internship hosted by a local church.

The pilot program aimed to aid post-matriculant youth in skills development (both soft and hard), job readiness, and job-hunting skills. As someone who had recently joined the workforce, my task was to audit the program. Myself and a colleague gave feedback on what we found useful, what was missing, and what we thought ought to be changed.

This pilot led to the church developing a much more comprehensive program and also led to my being hired to manage the coffee shop in 2019.

2019 - 2020

Life Cafe

Towards the end of 2018, I was approached to manage a socially successful, but fiscally failing coffee shop.

Within four months, and with the help of some amazing people, we were able to break even and justify its continued existence.

On top various hard skills, I learned how to learn from people who know better than me (being the only new hire), and also how to win people over into a new way of thinking when “This is how we’ve always done it,” was no longer working.

2020 - 2022

KZN Print Media

Having learned what I could about turning a business around from Life Cafe, in 2020 I accepted the opportunity to be the first hire for a new printing company.
This was going to give me a hands-on learning experience with starting a new business venture.
I especially learned how to manage resources, handle customer relations, and unlike Life Cafe where I had very little oversight, I learned how to break down management goals into smaller executable tasks.
